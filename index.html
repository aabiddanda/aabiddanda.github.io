Research

I am currently a senior at Cornell University studying Computer Science. My interests are in taking methods from Machine Learning, Graph Theory, and Statistics and applying them to large-scale genomic problems.

Currently I am working in Alon Keinan's Lab studying the effects of sex-biased admixture and developing software to better analyze the X-chromosome in genome-wide association studies.


Software

I host most of my software on my GitHub page. A couple of interesting projects with genomic applications are below:

    An IBD analysis pipeline - useful for inferring haplotype blocks from unphased SNP data
    An ADMIXTURE plotting pipeline - Used in conjuction with ADMIXTURE and DISTRUCT for nicely plotting results
